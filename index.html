What is Chaupadi?

During menstruation, rural Nepalese women are kept away from their homes in a shed, called "chau" in Nepali. Women are considered "impure" during their menstruation. Women are unable to maintain their menstrual hygiene because these chaus are more than often unhygienic. In addition, menstruating women are prohibited from participating in day-to-day activities, social events and attending school. Snake bites, cold winters and hot summers, rape cases are only some of the events that take place in these filthy huts.

What Do We Do?

Affording menstrual hygeine can be expensive, especially in rural places of Nepal. We distribute sanitary pads completely for free in such areas.

It is difficult to explain to the community why Chaupadi is a malpractice due to the cultural importance it holds. We hold campaigns in such areas to bring awareness among people.

Illiteracy can be considered the leading cause of this social practice. We provide sex and menstruation education in rural schools to educate the younger generation.
